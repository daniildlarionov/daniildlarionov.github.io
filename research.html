Working papers

Full Surplus Extraction from Colluding Bidders (Job Market Paper, draft coming soon)

I consider a repeated auction setting with colluding buyers and a seller who adjusts reserve prices over time without long-term commitment. To model the seller's concern for collusion, I introduce a new equilibrium concept: collusive public perfect equilibrium (cPPE). For every strategy of the seller I define the corresponding "buyer-game" in which the seller is replaced by Nature who chooses the reserve prices for the buyers in accordance with the seller's strategy. A public perfect equilibrium is collusive if the buyers cannot achieve a higher symmetric public perfect equilibrium payoff in the corresponding buyer-game. In a setting with symmetric buyers with private binary iid valuations and publicly revealed bids, I find a seller-optimal stationary cPPE, that, in the limit as the discount factor goes to 1, allows the seller to extract the entire surplus from the buyers. I therefore show that a patient, non-committed seller can effectively fight collusion even when she can only set reserve prices and has to satisfy stringent public disclosure requirements.

First Best Implementation with Costly Information Acquisition
joint with Hien Pham and Takuro Yamashita

We study a mechanism design model with flexible but costly information acquisition. There is a principal and I ≥ 4 agents. The principal and the agents share a common prior over the set of payoff-relevant states of the world. The principal proposes a mechanism to the agents who can then acquire information about the state of the world by privately designing a signal device. As long as it is costless for each agent to acquire a signal that is pairwise independent from the state of the world, we show that there exists a mechanism which allows the principal to implement any social choice rule at zero information acquisition cost to the agents.

Common Value Auctions with Costly Information Acquisition
joint with Takuro Yamashita

We study an auction design problem with pure common values and flexible but costly information acquisition. There are buyers and a seller who has a single unit of a good for sale. In the beginning, the seller and the buyers share a common prior over a binary set of values. The seller proposes an auction to the buyers, who can then acquire information about the good's value by privately designing a signal device. We show that, regardless of the functional form of the information acquisition cost, the seller can achieve full surplus extraction when there are I ≥ 3 buyers or when the lower value in the support of the common prior is non-positive; but cannot achieve full surplus extraction with I=2 buyers when both values are strictly positive. Assuming that the information cost is proportional to the expected reduction in entropy, we characterize the set of implementable allocations and solve for an optimal auction with I=2 buyers and strictly positive values.
